  • Delivered energy

    This is the amount of usable energy that is consumed by a facility, expressed in energy units. This would include the metered amounts of electricity, natural gas and other fuels supplied to and used by a facility.

  • Primary energy

    This is the amount of energy expressed in its original unconverted form and before considering any transmission and distribution losses. E.g. electricity from the grid has to be generated from the combustion of natural gas, oil, renewables, etc., and then transmitted and distributed to consumers through the supply grid.

  • Carbon dioxide (CO2e) conversion factor

    This is the amount of greenhouse gases emitted (carbon dioxide, methane and nitrous oxides), expressed as total carbon dioxide equivalent (CO2e), for each unit of energy consumed.

  • Primary energy conversion factor

    This is the amount of primary energy consumed to produce each unit of delivered energy. For most fuel types, the primary conversion factor is 1. For electricity from the grid, the primary conversion factor accounts for the efficiency of generating, transmitting and distributing each delivered unit of energy. The factor is calculated based on UK energy data from 2022 and reflects the average mix of UK electricity generation and the typical transmission and distribution losses.
